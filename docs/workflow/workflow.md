**"Why do I need a workflow?"**

**Efficiency.**

Would you rather take 6 months on a project or just a few weeks?

Think of is as a checklist. An order of operations that helps you build out your project efficiently, and with minimal backtracking.

Pilots use checklists everyday to ensure their plane is safe for takeoff, and that list is the same, every single time. It makes sure that nothing is missed, and the process gets faster every iteration.

Luckily in 3D animation, lives aren't at stake. But we can still use that methodology to assist us.

In this section, we go over a suggested order of operations and tips in each of them. Animation is an art and a science, we can only give you the "science" part of this. What you decide to do, what you WANT to do, is the "art" side.

**1. File Management**: The first thing you need to figure out is where things are going to go. Decide where you are placing your project file, your audio cues, and where your renders are going to be placed.

**2. Camera & Framing**: Plan out how you are going to frame your characters. Figure out timings, placements, etc.

**3. Animation**: Animate your characters! You can start with keyframes and then do in-betweens, or whatever you feel comfortable with.

**4. Lighting**: Lighting makes up maybe 80% of how much you like the final product. Important to have a "main light" and keep it consistent unless you're going for something more stylistic.

**5. Rendering**: Rendering is your computer taking ALL the data that you can in the shot, and putting it together frame by frame to give you what you want. This is highly customizable to what you are wanting from the next step.

**6. Compositing**: The "post-processing" this can be as deep or as non-existant as you wish. It can definitely help with giving you that "artistic touch" to your projects!
